## Key Principles

1. **Five distinct lede types**, each suited to different story types:
   - **Hard/Summary**: 5W+H in first paragraph. Breaking news, wire copy, time-sensitive.
   - **Anecdotal**: Mini-story (3-5 paragraphs) of one person/moment that embodies the broader theme. Features, longform, investigations.
   - **Scene-setting**: Vivid place/moment description. Sets atmosphere. Features, profiles, place-based stories.
   - **Blind/Delayed**: Withholds the "who" or "what" to build tension. Reveals in nut graph. Investigations, mysteries, surprises.
   - **Question**: Rhetorical question that the story answers. Risk of cliche. Use sparingly.

2. **Length**: 35-40 words max for hard ledes. Anecdotal/scene ledes can run 3-5 paragraphs but MUST lead to a nut graph.

3. **Selection rule**: Match lede type to your strongest reporting element. If you have a powerful person/moment, go anecdotal. If you have a shocking number, go hard. If the place IS the story, go scene-setting.

4. **"The promise"**: An effective lede makes a promise: "I have something important, something interesting, to tell you." It beckons and invites.

5. **Anti-patterns**: Cliched language, rambling sentences, irrelevant detail, direct questions to reader, starting with "In recent years" or date-first constructions.
