# Research: Narrative Arc Tension

## Sources
- North American Review: "Narrative Tension: The Promises Writers Make"
- Fiveable: Pacing and Rhythm in Storytelling
- Heinemann: Creating Tension in Nonfiction
- Literary Devices: How to Build Tension in Writing
- Various pacing guides (authority.pub, jerichowriters.com)

## Key Principles

1. **The Question Engine**: Every section answers one question and raises the next. The reader always has an unanswered question pulling them forward. If there's no open question, there's no tension. This is the core mechanism: plant question → withhold answer → deliver answer while planting the next question.

2. **Tension ≠ speed**: A story isn't good because it's fast; it's good because it's tense. Tension comes from unanswered questions and unresolved stakes, not from short sentences or rapid pacing. In fact, slowing down at the right moment INCREASES tension (the pause before the answer).

3. **Pacing through sentence length**: Short sentences create urgency. Long sentences invite contemplation. The rhythm of alternation mirrors a heartbeat. But this must be strategic: short sentences at moments of revelation or surprise, long sentences during scene-setting or explanation.

4. **Stakes escalation**: Each section should raise the stakes slightly. "This is bad" → "This is worse than we thought" → "And here's what happens if we do nothing." The reader's sense of urgency should build, not plateau.

5. **The tension-release cycle**: Constant tension exhausts. Alternate between tension (unanswered question, rising stakes) and release (answer delivered, moment of clarity, human detail). The release earns the next escalation. Feature profiles do this naturally: scene (tension) → reflection/data (release) → scene (tension).

6. **Nonfiction tension sources**: In nonfiction, tension comes from (a) unanswered questions about outcome, (b) conflict between forces/positions, (c) the gap between what should be and what is, (d) time pressure (deadlines, deadlines, deadlines), (e) the reader's empathy with a protagonist whose outcome is uncertain.
