# Research: Narrative Arc Structures

## Sources
- Poynter: "The Hourglass" (Roy Peter Clark, 2003)
- Fiveable: Narrative Structures study guide
- CWI Pressbooks: Newswriting Structures
- Wikipedia: Article structure
- LibreTexts: Story Structure (6.4)

## Key Structures

### 1. Inverted Pyramid
- Most important facts first, supporting details in descending order
- Reader can stop at any point and still be informed
- Best for: hard news, breaking events, wire copy, data-driven reports
- Weakness: no narrative pull after the lede. Reader has no reason to continue.

### 2. Hourglass (Roy Peter Clark, 1983)
- THREE PARTS: Top (summary, 4-6 paragraphs) → Turn (transitional phrase) → Narrative (chronological story)
- "If all that is read is the top, the reader is still informed"
- The Turn uses attribution phrases: "according to police," "eyewitnesses described"
- The Narrative shows what the top merely tells
- Best for: dramatic stories with chronological action (crime, business, government)
- Key advantage: serves both the scanner (top) and the reader (narrative)

### 3. Diamond
- Anecdote → Nut graph → Broadening with data/context → Convergence back to the opening story
- "Begins narrow, widens, then narrows again"
- Best for: feature profiles, opinion journalism, magazine-style pieces
- Essentially: anecdotal lede → zoom out → zoom back in

### 4. Braided/Parallel Narrative
- Two or more storylines woven together, alternating between them
- Creates tension through juxtaposition (e.g., the scientist AND the melting ice)
- Best for: complex stories with multiple timelines or perspectives
- Risk: reader confusion if transitions between threads are unclear

## Key Principle

**"Form follows content."** Determine what the story needs to convey before selecting the structural container. Don't force a narrative structure onto a story that wants to be an inverted pyramid.

## Selection Rule
- Breaking news → Inverted Pyramid
- Dramatic event with chronological action → Hourglass
- Feature/profile with a central character → Diamond
- Complex story with multiple timelines/perspectives → Braided
- Data-heavy investigation → Inverted Pyramid or Diamond (nut graph critical)
