# Research: Scene-Setting Economy

## Sources
- Nieman Storyboard: "Six Writing Tips for Crafting Scenes" (Laurie Hertzel)
- Nieman Storyboard: "Reporting and writing scenes" (Lauren Kessler)
- No Film School: "Economy in Storytelling"
- Fiveable: Brevity in journalism

## Key Principles

1. **"Stepping stones, not a sidewalk."** (Hertzel) Scenes are selective moments, not continuous coverage. The reader will follow you across gaps. You don't have to show every step. Use scene for pivotal moments; use summary to bridge between them.

2. **Fold description into action.** Don't halt the narrative with descriptive blocks. Instead of "The kitchen was small and outdated. The counters were laminate." → "She ran her hand along the laminate counter, doing the math out loud." The description is embedded in the character's action.

3. **Camera angle is a conscious choice.** Are you zoomed in (intimate, one person, one detail) or pulled back (panoramic, establishing shot)? Unclear positioning confuses the reader. Pick one per scene.

4. **Enter late, exit early.** Start the scene at the moment of tension, not the build-up. End before full resolution to maintain forward momentum. "Provide a sense of completion without closure so strong that readers stop." (Hertzel)

5. **Scene length matches story significance.** Slow down for crucial dramatic moments (expand a single action across multiple sentences). Speed up for context and transition (summarize in one sentence). The amount of space you give a moment signals its importance to the reader.
