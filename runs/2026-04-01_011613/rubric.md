# Scoring Rubric — Dark Money Op-Ed

## Dimensions

### 1. Argument Strength (weight: 0.30)
**Definition**: How persuasively the piece builds its case from Thiel's funding to candidates' anti-Israel positions to GOP electoral strategy.
- **1-2**: No clear thesis. Claims float without connection. Counterarguments ignored.
- **5-6**: Thesis present. Evidence chain has gaps. Counterarguments acknowledged but not handled.
- **9-10**: Thesis sharp and sustained. Evidence builds paragraph by paragraph. Strongest counterargument addressed directly and defused.

### 2. Specificity (weight: 0.25)
**Definition**: Named PACs, dollar amounts, dates, FEC filings, and concrete events replace vague claims.
- **1-2**: "Thiel donated a lot of money." No numbers, no PAC names, no dates.
- **5-6**: Some dollar figures and PAC names. Missing key details (Per Aspera, FEC records).
- **9-10**: Every claim anchored to a specific dollar amount, PAC name, date, or FEC filing. Reader can verify.

### 3. Voice & Register (weight: 0.25)
**Definition**: Op-ed lexicon compliance. Text sounds like a real opinion columnist, not an AI. Lexicon-sourced synonyms used naturally.
- **1-2**: Corporate memo tone. Kill-list words throughout. No editorial voice.
- **5-6**: Op-ed voice emerging but inconsistent. Some AI-tell patterns remain. Lexicon words feel forced.
- **9-10**: Reads like a published op-ed. Zero kill-list words. Lexicon synonyms invisible — reader wouldn't notice they were substituted.

### 4. Structure (weight: 0.20)
**Definition**: Point-first paragraphs, logical flow from thesis to evidence to implication, strong kicker.
- **1-2**: Buries the lede. Paragraphs wander. No kicker.
- **5-6**: Lede present. Some paragraphs point-first. Kicker exists but weak.
- **9-10**: Every paragraph opens with its claim. Evidence follows claim. Kicker lands with conviction. Reader knows exactly what the author believes and why.
